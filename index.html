PHYSICAL PROCESS
DENTAL IMPRESSION
Client bites into alginate impression material. Hold 2–3 min without moving. Pour dental stone immediately — alginate distorts when dry. Cure 45–60 min.
Mix alginate to thick paste consistency — too thin and it won't hold shape
Client must not move during bite — one shift ruins the entire mold
Remove impression in one clean pull — no wiggling or rocking
Pour dental stone IMMEDIATELY — alginate distorts fast when exposed to air
Label every model with client name + date — never mix up orders
+
3
DIGITIZING
SCAN THE MODEL
Spray stone model with matte scanning spray. Scan with Revopoint MINI 2. Clean up scan in Revopoint software. Export as STL or OBJ file.
Matte scanning spray prevents reflection that confuses the scanner (~$15)
Use turntable if scanner supports it — more complete capture
Save scan file as: ClientName_Date_Top/Bottom.stl
Alternative if no scanner yet: measure model manually and sculpt freehand in Nomad
+
4
DESIGN — iPAD PRO
DESIGN IN NOMAD SCULPT
Import STL into Nomad Sculpt on iPad Pro. Sculpt grill design over the teeth model. Export as STL when complete. Requires Apple Pencil Pro.
Nomad Sculpt is iOS/iPadOS ONLY — does not run on Mac
Import client's tooth STL as reference, sculpt grill over it
Design styles: flooded, open face, fangs, cutouts, textures, diamond dust
Export as STL — save both the Nomad project file AND the STL
+
5
PREP — MAC MINI
SLICE ON MAC MINI
Open STL in ELEGOOSlicer or Chitubox (both free, both run on Mac). Add supports, configure castable resin settings. Export .ctb file to Elegoo Mars 5.
Use ELEGOOSlicer or Chitubox — both free, both Mac native, no Windows needed
Castable resin requires specific exposure settings — check Siraya Tech's recommended settings
Add supports carefully — avoid placing on visible surfaces
Transfer .ctb file to printer via USB or SD card
+
6
FABRICATION
PRINT ON ELEGOO MARS 5
Print using castable wax resin (Siraya Tech or Elegoo castable). Wash in IPA. UV cure. Inspect for layer issues, thin spots, or print failures.
Use castable resin — not standard resin, it must burn out cleanly for casting
Wash in IPA for 3–5 minutes, cure under UV lamp for recommended time
Inspect every print under bright light before sending to caster
Failed prints: check for delamination, warping, or unsupported areas
+
7
PRODUCTION
SEND TO CASTER
Wrap print in tissue, pack tight with no movement, ship with tracking. Email caster before it arrives with metal specs, karat, and finish preferences.
Photograph everything before shipping — for your records and in case of damage
Always confirm caster accepts resin prints before your first shipment
Include: metal type, karat, any special finish instructions in your email
Compare both casters on price, turnaround, and quality before committing to one
+
8
RECEIVING
RECEIVE & CLEAN CASTINGS
Inspect raw castings for porosity, sprue marks, surface quality. Remove sprues, file rough areas. Light cleanup and polish for photography.
Surface porosity (tiny holes) is normal — polishing handles most surface issues
Major defects: photograph and contact caster immediately
Start with coarser files and work progressively to finer grits
Be gentle — you cannot put metal back once it is removed

Current: clean, polish lightly, deliver with remaining balance collected
Future: rotary polishing tool, polishing compounds, in-house stone setting
Photograph final piece before delivery — add to portfolio

04
2–4 HRS / DAY · EVERY DAY

4-WEEK GRIND

WEEK 1
WARMUP & FOUNDATIONS
Get your hands back in rhythm. Focus on consistency over perfection. Goal: 2 clean stone models poured + wax applied to both.
DAY 1
MONDAY
SETUP + FIRST IMPRESSION
▾
HOUR 1
Set Up Your Workspace
Lay out all impression materials. Organize trays by size. Mix a small test batch of alginate to check your water ratio before committing a tray.
HOURS 2–3
First Impression — Bottom Arch
Do it on yourself. Mix alginate, load tray, bite down firm and still for 2–3 minutes. Pour dental stone immediately. Let sit 45–60 min to cure.

Don't move while biting — one shift ruins the mold

Pour stone while alginate is still wet

REMAINING TIME
Build Your Reference Folder
Pull up grillz Instagram accounts and Pinterest. Save references for flooded, open face, and fang styles. Know what you're building toward.
DAY 2
TUESDAY
WAX APPLICATION
▾
HOUR 1
Inspect Yesterday's Model
Remove stone model carefully. Check for air bubbles, cracks, or distortion. Note what went well and what to fix next time.
HOURS 2–4
First Flooded Bottom 6
Apply wax to the bottom 6 teeth. Focus on even thickness, clean gumline, smooth surface. This is your benchmark piece — treat it like a paying order.

Keep wax thickness consistent — thin spots crack during casting

Use dental tools to smooth edges and define the gumline

DAY 3
WEDNESDAY
TOP ARCH
▾
HOUR 1
Pour Top Arch Impression
Take a top arch impression. Apply everything learned from Day 1. Focus on a cleaner pour with fewer air bubbles.
HOURS 2–4
Continue Bottom 6 Refinement
Go back to yesterday's wax up. Refine edges, smooth rough areas, and work on the inner channel — the part that sits on the teeth.

The inner channel fit is everything — this determines client comfort

DAY 4
THURSDAY
TOP 6 WAX UP
▾
HOURS 1–4
Flooded Top 6
Apply wax to the top arch. Top teeth have different proportions — wider, flatter. Adjust thickness and gumline accordingly. Photo both pieces side by side when done.

Top front teeth are wider and flatter — don't treat them the same as bottom

DAY 5
FRIDAY
OPEN FACE INTRO
▾
HOURS 1–2
Study Open Face Technique
Watch tutorials on open face wax technique. Note how windows are cut, how edges are finished, what tools are used. Don't touch wax yet — just absorb.
HOURS 3–4
First Open Face Attempt
Take your bottom 6 flooded piece and attempt open face windows. Don't worry about perfection. Focus on clean window edges and consistent frame width.

Cut windows slightly smaller than you think — remove more later if needed

DAY 6
SATURDAY
CRITIQUE + REDO
▾
HOUR 1
Self Critique Session
Photograph all your wax work from the week. Look critically — where is wax uneven? Where are edges rough? Where does the gumline dip? Write it down.
HOURS 2–4
Redo Your Weakest Piece
Pick whichever piece had the most issues and redo it from scratch. Redoing is faster than fixing — and teaches more than any tutorial.

Repetition is the skill. Every redo makes the next one cleaner.

DAY 7
SUNDAY
REST + RESEARCH
▾
LIGHT DAY — 1–2 HRS
Research & Plan Week 2
No wax today. Research open face techniques, cutout ideas, fang designs. Build your inspiration folder. Plan which designs you want to attempt in Week 2.

Rest days are part of the process — come back fresh on Monday

WEEK 2
REFINE & REPEAT
Build consistency. Every piece this week should be better than last week. Goal: 1 clean flooded set + 1 clean open face set. Contact both casters.
DAY 8
MONDAY
FRESH IMPRESSIONS + CASTERS
▾
HOURS 1–2
Pour 2 Fresh Impressions
Take impressions from a friend or family member — different teeth than yours exposes you to different arch sizes and tooth shapes, exactly like client work.
HOURS 3–4
Contact Both Casters
Reach out to both casters today. Ask: do they accept resin prints, metals available, turnaround, pricing, and how to ship. Get everything in writing.

Ask if they handle spruing themselves or if you need to attach sprues

Compare both before committing to either

DAY 9
TUESDAY
BEST FLOODED YET
▾
HOURS 1–4
Your Best Flooded Bottom 6
Using the fresh model — make your cleanest flooded bottom 6 yet. Slow down. Focus on every edge. This is a potential portfolio piece. Treat it like it's for a paying client.

Take photos at every stage — before, during, and after

DAY 10
WEDNESDAY
OPEN FACE MASTERY
▾
HOURS 1–4
Clean Open Face Set
Build a full open face bottom 6 from start to finish. Consistent window sizing across all teeth, razor clean frame edges. This should significantly top your Week 1 attempt.

Use a template or guide to keep window sizes consistent tooth to tooth

DAY 11
THURSDAY
TOP ARCH OPEN FACE
▾
HOURS 1–4
Open Face Top 6
Apply open face technique to the top arch. Top teeth behave differently — wider windows, flatter surface. Adjust your approach and document the difference between arches.
DAY 12
FRIDAY
BOTTOM 8 CHALLENGE
▾
HOURS 1–4
Extend to Bottom 8
Most clients want 8 teeth covered. Practice extending wax to include the two premolars. The transition from front teeth to side teeth is a common weak point — master it.

The side teeth curve — your wax needs to follow the arch, not fight it

DAY 13
SATURDAY
PHOTO + SELECT FOR CASTING
▾
HOURS 1–2
Photograph Your Best Pieces
Clean background, good lighting. Photograph your best wax pieces. These go in your portfolio even before casting.
HOURS 3–4
Select Pieces to Cast
Pick your 2–3 best pieces across both weeks to send to the caster once pricing is confirmed. Mark them, store safely, don't touch them again.
DAY 14
SUNDAY
REST + NOMAD PREP
▾
LIGHT DAY
Start Learning Nomad Sculpt
Watch intro Nomad Sculpt tutorials today — basic navigation, sculpting tools, importing STL files. Get familiar before you have the device in hand.

YouTube: search "Nomad Sculpt beginner" — lots of free tutorials

WEEK 3
CUSTOM DESIGNS
Push beyond standard styles. Cutouts, textures, fangs, abstract work. Goal: 1 custom cutout + 1 fang set started. Send pieces to caster.
DAY 15
MONDAY
CUSTOM CUTOUT
▾
HOUR 1
Sketch Your Cutout Design
On paper first — design a shape to carve into a flooded piece. Stars, hearts, initials, symbols. Keep it simple for the first attempt.
HOURS 2–4
Execute the Custom Cutout
Build a flooded piece then carefully carve your custom design — more deliberate and refined than the heart from your early practice. Document every step.

Use the smallest dental tool for detail cuts

Work under good lighting — shadows hide mistakes

DAY 16
TUESDAY
TEXTURE WORK
▾
HOURS 1–4
Diamond Dust & Abstract Texture
Practice adding surface texture to a flooded piece. Diamond dust effect, geometric patterns, or abstract scoring. Texture adds huge visual interest and perceived value.

Diamond dust is done by pressing a textured surface into soft wax

Consistent pressure = consistent texture depth

DAY 17
WEDNESDAY
FANG RESEARCH
▾
HOURS 1–2
Deep Dive on Fang Technique
Fangs extend beyond the natural tooth — different build entirely. Study how the extension is constructed, supported, and finished. Watch multiple tutorials before wax.
HOURS 3–4
Mock Fang Build — No Pressure
First attempt at building fang extensions. Focus on the attachment point where fang meets natural tooth outline. This is the hardest part structurally.
DAY 18
THURSDAY
FULL FANG SET
▾
HOURS 1–4
Complete Fang Set
Build a full fang set — both canines extended. Focus on symmetry, consistent length, and smooth taper from base to tip.

Measure fang length on both sides before finishing — asymmetry is very noticeable

Fangs need enough thickness at the base to survive casting

DAY 19
FRIDAY
COMBINED DESIGN
▾
HOURS 1–4
Open Face + Custom Detail
Combine two techniques in one piece — open face frame with a custom detail element. Cutout, texture, or engraving. This is a real sellable design concept.
DAY 20
SATURDAY
SEND TO CASTER
▾
HOURS 1–2
Final Piece Selection
Select your best 2–3 pieces across all weeks. Inspect for thin spots, cracks, or incomplete areas before shipping. These represent Noire Grillz.
HOURS 3–4
Pack & Ship
Wrap individually in tissue, pack snug, ship with tracking. Email caster your metal preferences before the package arrives.

Photograph everything before shipping — your records and proof of condition

DAY 21
SUNDAY
REST + NOMAD
▾
LIGHT DAY
Nomad Sculpt Exploration
If your iPad has arrived — spend today getting comfortable in Nomad. No pressure to make anything good. Just explore tools, sculpt random shapes, feel the Apple Pencil.
WEEK 4
PORTFOLIO READY
Castings arrive. Polish, photograph, post, open orders. Goal: real metal pieces in your portfolio and first clients lined up.
DAY 22
MONDAY
RECEIVE CASTINGS
▾
WHEN THEY ARRIVE
Inspect Raw Castings
Examine every piece carefully. Note casting quality, surface defects, porosity. This feedback shapes your relationship with the caster going forward.

Porosity (tiny holes) is normal — polishing handles surface issues

Major defects: contact caster immediately with photos

DAY 23
TUESDAY
BASIC CLEANUP
▾
HOURS 1–4
Clean Up Raw Castings
Remove sprue marks, file rough edges, basic cleanup on each piece. Not final polish yet — getting pieces presentable for photography stage.

Start coarse, work to finer grits progressively

Be gentle — you cannot add metal back

DAY 24
WEDNESDAY
POLISH
▾
HOURS 1–4
Polish Your Best Piece
Bring one piece to high shine for your hero portfolio photo. Work through polishing compounds progressively. This piece IS Noire Grillz — make it shine.

A rotary tool with polishing wheel speeds this up significantly

DAY 25
THURSDAY
PHOTO SHOOT
▾
HOURS 1–2
Set Up Your Shot
Black velvet background, macro lens or iPhone portrait mode, natural window light or ring light. Clean every piece with a soft cloth before shooting.
HOURS 3–4
Shoot All Portfolio Pieces
Multiple angles, closeups of details, and on-model shots if possible. These go on Instagram, your website, and your order form.

On-model (in-mouth) shots always perform best on social media

DAY 26
FRIDAY
LAUNCH
▾
HOURS 1–4
Post + Open Orders
Edit your best photos, post to Instagram, announce Noire Grillz is taking orders. Update order form with finalized deposit amount and pricing.

Don't wait until everything is perfect — done is better than perfect when launching

First clients will come from people who already know you

DAY 27
SATURDAY
NOMAD SCULPT
▾
HOURS 1–4
First Digital Grill Design
In Nomad Sculpt — attempt your first digital grill. Search "teeth STL free download" for a practice model. Sculpt a basic flooded set over it.

Don't compare digital to physical yet — different skills, same destination

DAY 28
SUNDAY
REFLECT + PLAN
▾
LIGHT DAY
Review the 4 Weeks 🔥
Look back at Day 1 vs now. What improved? What still needs work? Write your top 3 strengths and top 3 areas to keep developing. This becomes your Month 2 focus. Real metal pieces. Portfolio live. Orders open. Digital workflow started. Noire Grillz is moving.

💰
PRICING STRUCTURE
  • Base cost = resin + casting + labor + markup
  • Flooded 6: metal weight × casting rate + margin
  • Open face: base + design premium
  • Fangs: base + fang extension premium
  • Diamond dust / custom: base + custom premium
  • 10k < 14k < 18k — price each karat accordingly
03
💳
PAYMENTS
  • Set up Cash App, Zelle, or Square before first order
  • 50% deposit at order — non-refundable
  • Remaining 50% before delivery
  • All sales final — already on your order form
  • Never start work without deposit in hand
04
📸
PORTFOLIO & MARKETING
  • Cast best practice wax pieces for real metal portfolio photos
  • On-model (in-mouth) shots perform best on Instagram
  • Black velvet background + ring light or window light
  • Post consistently — first clients come from your network
  • Always ask: "Want to be featured on Instagram?"
05
🤝
CASTER CHECKLIST
  • Do you accept castable resin prints?
  • Metals available: 10k, 14k, 18k, silver, rose gold?
  • Pricing per gram or per piece by metal?
  • Standard turnaround time?
  • Do you handle spruing?
  • How to pack and ship resin prints?

  • No Windows machine needed for this workflow
  • Export from Nomad as STL for printing
